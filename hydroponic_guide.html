Seedling Thinning & Separation
Days 5–14 · What to do with your extras
⏳
Best timing: Wait until roots are just peeking out the bottom of the pod — plants have enough individual root structure to survive separation cleanly.

Choosing which seedling to keep

Keep the seedling that is most upright, has the thickest stem, the most evenly-colored leaves, and whose roots have grown deepest into the pod. Discard stretched/leggy, pale, or misshapen seedlings.

Separation technique

  1. 1Remove the pod and hold over a bowl of pH'd water.
  2. 2Use a toothpick to gently tease the sponge apart around the base of the seedling to remove.
  3. 3Wiggle gently side-to-side while lifting — never pull straight up.
  4. 4If roots are badly tangled: cut between stems with small scissors rather than risk damaging both plants.
  5. 5Move the removed seedling within 60–90 seconds — roots dry out fast under grow lights.

Options for removed seedlings

~70–80% survival

🪴 Replant into empty pod

Pre-soak a fresh pod in 25% nutrient solution before removing the seedling. Seat with stem base at pod top, roots going down. Mist leaves twice daily for 2–3 days after transplant. Keep water level slightly higher than normal for those first few days.

Up to 24 hours

🥤 Float in a cup (emergency)

Sit the seedling in a small cup of 25% nutrient solution — roots submerged, stem and leaves dry. Place in indirect light. Buys time while you prep a proper pod.

Lettuce: easy · Tomato: doable

🌿 Transfer to soil

Use light, well-draining seedling mix. Keep consistently moist for the first week. Keep out of direct sun for 3–4 days while roots adjust to soil conditions.

Lettuce only

🥗 Microgreens (zero waste)

Lay extras on a damp paper towel or shallow tray with a thin layer of water. Harvest as tender microgreens in 3–4 days. Cotyledons and young true leaves are edible.

Pruning — Lettuce & Arugula (iDoo)
Minimal intervention · No topping · Arugula bolts faster
ActionWhenHow
Remove cotyledons (seed leaves)Day 10–14, when yellowing naturallyPinch off cleanly at the base
Remove leaves touching waterAny time you notice themPinch off — prevents bacterial rot in reservoir
Outer leaf harvest — lettuce (cut-and-come-again)Day 28+ when leaves are full sized (4–5")Cut outer leaves, leave center rosette intact
Outer leaf harvest — arugula (cut-and-come-again)Day 21+ at 3–5"; harvest earlier and more often than lettuceCut outer leaves, leave center rosette intact
Full harvest before boltingWhen center stalk elongates rapidly — arugula bolts faster than lettuceCut whole plant at base immediately — taste degrades fast
🚫
Do not top or pinch the center growing point of young lettuce. Lettuce grows as a rosette from a single central point — damage there stunts the whole plant permanently.
🥬
Outer leaves at 3–4"? Leave them — they're almost at harvest size but still feeding the plant. Wait until they reach 4–5" or start drooping toward the water before cutting. Take the 2–3 outermost only, and the plant will keep pushing new growth from the center.
🔄
Succession planting tip: When rotating young seedlings into front/center pod positions and moving mature plants to the back, do it right after a reservoir change when plants are well-hydrated. Ease pods straight up slowly — roots can be long and tangled toward the water. Once mature plants are at the back, do a more aggressive outer-leaf harvest to reduce shading on the new seedlings.

2-stem system for desktop hydroponics

Keep the main central stem and the first strong sucker that grows just below the first flower cluster. Remove all other suckers as they appear. More than 2 stems in a desktop system creates competition for light and nutrients.

Identifying suckers vs. branches

🔍
Sucker: Grows at ~45° from the V-junction between main stem and a leaf · soft slightly pale green tip.
Branch: Grows from a node · slightly firmer base.

When in doubt, wait. A sucker left a few more days gets bigger, easier to identify, and still comes off cleanly.
✅
Golden rule for both systems: If a leaf is green and alive, it's working — leave it. Only remove what is yellow, dying, touching water, or a clearly identified sucker on tomatoes.

Using pH Up and Down

Always adjust the already-mixed nutrient solution — not plain water. Add pH Down drop by drop (it's concentrated acid). Stir thoroughly and wait 30 seconds before re-testing. pH tends to drift upward in active systems — expect to use pH Down more often than pH Up.
