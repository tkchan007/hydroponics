Germination — The Key to Success
  • Cold stratification (best method): Place seeds in a damp paper towel, seal in a bag, refrigerate for 3–5 days BEFORE planting. Breaks dormancy significantly.
  • Alternative: Soak in room-temp water for 24 hours before planting
  • Light requirement: Seeds need light to germinate — press onto grow media surface, do NOT bury
  • Temperature: Keep above 68°F — below that and germination can take 30+ days
  • Patience: 14–21 days is normal. Don't give up on pods that seem slow — they often surprise you
Growth Timeline
Days 1–5 (pre-plant)
Cold stratification in fridge. Then soak 24 hrs before planting day.
Week 1–3
Waiting period. Germination 14–21 days. Keep moist and warm. Don't overwater the pod.
Week 3–4
First true leaves appear — distinctive round-serrated shape, purple tint on undersides.
Week 4–6
Plant grows rapidly. Pinch the top growing tip at 3–4" to encourage bushy branching.
Week 6–8
🎉 Harvest! Pinch top 2–3 leaf pairs. Very aromatic at this stage.
Week 8 onward
Ongoing harvests every 1–2 weeks. Pinch any flower buds immediately — bolting ends harvest life.

Tips & Watch-Outs

Key Compatibility Notes
  • Water Spinach is the odd one out temperature-wise. It needs 70–78°F water while all the others prefer 65–72°F. Growing it alongside cool-preference crops means compromising for both. Keep it as a standalone crop on the Suncore when possible, or run it in summer when ambient temps rise naturally.
  • Best iDoo combination: Baby Bok Choy + Perilla. Nearly identical light, temperature, pH, and EC preferences. Plant them together with confidence — stagger the pods so bok choy doesn't shade the slower-starting perilla.
  • Best Suncore combination: Tong Hao + Red Amaranth. Both love the same conditions, have similar EC needs, and their harvest schedules complement each other. Tong Hao is cut-and-come-again while amaranth fills the same nutritional role if one crop is between cycles.
  • Pea Shoots are solo by nature. They're harvested so fast (10–14 days) that they're essentially in and out before their neighbors hit stride. Run them as a separate cycle — plant a new batch every 2 weeks in dedicated pods.
  • EC compromise for mixed pods: If you're running bok choy (EC 1.5–2.2) alongside perilla (EC 1.0–1.6), target EC 1.5 — the low end for bok choy and the high end for perilla. Both will thrive.
Recommended System Layouts

🌱 iDoo 12-pod — Suggested Layout

  • Pods 1–4: Baby Bok Choy (dwarf)
  • Pods 5–7: Purple Perilla
  • Pods 8–9: Green Perilla
  • Pods 10–12: Pea Shoots (rotate every 2 wks)

🌿 Suncore 20-pod — Suggested Layout

  • Pods 1–6: Tong Hao
  • Pods 7–12: Red Amaranth
  • Pods 13–17: Water Spinach (when warm)
  • Pods 18–20: Reserve / succession planting
